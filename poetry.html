Poetry

Poems & small songs

Written in the margins of ordinary days.

I am learning the slow grammar of waiting —
how still is a verb, and hush is a kind of prayer.
How even the lilies, before they bloom,
must spend a long winter in the dark
becoming.
— Maggie, from "A small psalm for the in-between"
Maggie Lands

A quiet place to land, between the loud things.
